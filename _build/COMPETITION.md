## Detailed profiles

### 1. Ares (ares-app.com) — DIRECT COMPETITOR

**Company**: Bresca Digital SCCL, Mollerussa, Lleida, Spain. Catalan cooperative, founded 2023. Core business is digital consulting, Odoo installs, web design, Kit Digital subsidies. Ares is a side product, not main line. Funded via EU Next Generation + INAEM grant.

**What it does**: Internal operations platform for performing arts companies. Gig/event management, worker scheduling, material/vehicle tracking, availability confirmation, payroll pre-calculation (prenomines), Spanish Social Security (altas/bajas) report generation, basic CRM.

**Target**: Small-to-medium Spanish performing arts companies that need HR paperwork + scheduling in order.

**Pricing**:
- LITE: 30 €/mo (1 admin, 100 MB, basic features)
- GENERAL: 60 €/mo (unlimited admins, 1 GB, + calendar conflicts, payroll, stats)
- Setup: 150 € one-time
- Paid add-ons: Google Calendar sync 15 €/mo (!), Telegram availability 10 €/mo, template customization 25 €/ea

**Key weakness vs Hour**: No difusion/booking outreach, no sales pipeline, no tech rider management, no tour logistics, no multi-country support, no serious storage. Google Calendar as paid add-on in 2026 is embarrassing.

**Traction**: Presented at Mostra Igualada. No public customer testimonials after ~2 years. No press coverage. Weak.

**Threat level**: Medium. Only direct competitor in the Spanish performing arts niche, but limited to labor compliance. Could coexist — users might run Ares + Hour.

### 2. Stagent (stagent.com) — HIGH THREAT

**Company**: Founded by Mischa Sigtermans (DJ/entrepreneur). Netherlands-based. Joint venture with Artwin Software (market leader in Dutch booking). Recent investment secured for expansion beyond Netherlands.

**What it does**: All-in-one booking agency and artist management platform. Contract creation with built-in e-signature (own software, not third-party), invoicing with European e-invoicing standard compliance, tour planning, press kit builder, multi-artist event management, payment tracking.

**Target**: Music booking agencies and artist managers, primarily European.

**Pricing**:
- Starter: €99/mo (up to 10 artists)
- Enterprise: €799/mo (unlimited artists, API, dedicated success manager)
- Free plan for every artist invited by their agency
- Supports EU e-invoicing standards

**Key features relevant to Hour**: European compliance built-in, contract + invoice flow, multi-artist booking within same event/contract, Zapier integration (5000+ apps), iOS/Android apps with push notifications, unlimited team members.

**Key weakness vs Hour**: Music-agency-centric, not performing arts company perspective. No production management, no tech rider, no rehearsal scheduling. But close enough to be dangerous if they expand scope.

**Traction**: Joint venture with Artwin gives them installed base in Netherlands. Belgian agencies (Next In Line) reportedly cut admin time in half after adopting.

**Threat level**: HIGH. European-native, proper invoicing/contracts, growing. If they expand beyond music agencies toward performing arts companies, they become the primary threat.

### 3. SystemOne (systemonesoftware.com) — HIGH THREAT

**Company**: Based in Berlin, Germany. Founded by "Bartho" and team.

**What it does**: End-to-end booking agency platform. Artist calendar management, financial tracking/invoicing, contract creation with e-signatures, advancing sessions with promoters, artist riders, mobile app for artists/crew, data analytics, regional tax compliance, exclusivity rules, double-booking prevention.

**Target**: Booking agencies of all sizes, globally. Includes performing arts agencies specifically (has a dedicated performing arts page).

**Pricing**:
- Range: €35 to €1,188/mo across 3 editions
- Base price + per-agent-seat model
- 10% annual discount
- No hidden costs for contracts, external stakeholder access

**Key strengths vs Hour**: Already serves **400+ agencies, 25k artists, 1.9M shows** — massive traction. Specifically markets to performing arts agencies. Advancing workflow is strong. Regional tax compliance built in.

**Key weakness vs Hour**: Agency perspective, not production company perspective. No production management, no tech rider management, no rehearsal/travel scheduling for the company's own team. Designed for the agent who books artists, not the company that IS the artist.

**Traction**: 400+ agencies worldwide. Strongest traction signal in the category.

**Threat level**: HIGH. The agency-side market leader. If a performing arts company's booker uses SystemOne, the company itself still needs something for internal ops — that's Hour's space. But SystemOne could expand.

### 4. ABOSS (abossmusic.com) — HIGH THREAT

**Company**: Amsterdam, Netherlands. Start-up. Seed funding from River Venture Partners.

**What it does**: "Operating system for artists and booking agencies in live music." Automated contracts/statements/workflows, deal structure handling (flat fees, guarantees vs percentages, bonuses, WHT, multi-currency), advancing dashboard, travel module (flights, hotels, ground transport), real-time revenue/commission dashboards, AI-powered task surfacing.

**Target**: Music booking agencies and artists, EU-focused.

**Pricing**:
- Starter: €131/mo (up to 10 calendars)
- Growth: €288/mo (up to 30 calendars)
- Scale: €522/mo (unlimited calendars)
- All plans: unlimited users (seats), no extra cost
- 10% annual discount

**Key strengths vs Hour**: Very complete deal/financial management (WHT, multi-currency, commission tracking). Travel module included. AI features (daily digests, agentic AI). Unlimited seats model. Amsterdam-based = EU DNA.

**Key weakness vs Hour**: Music-industry vocabulary and workflow. No production management. No tech rider. No rehearsal scheduling. Agency perspective, not company-as-artist perspective.

**Traction**: Not publicly disclosed. Seed-funded. Active product development with AI features suggests committed team.

**Threat level**: HIGH. Most feature-complete EU booking tool. Their travel module and deal structure handling are exactly what touring companies need. If they shift vocabulary from "artist" to "company," they're in Hour's lane.

### 5. Overture (bookingwithoverture.com) — HIGH THREAT

**Company**: Brighton, East Sussex, UK. 15 years in operation (founded ~2011).

**What it does**: Booking management for agents, managers, and promoters. Calendar management, contract/invoice generation with custom templates, e-signatures (Dropbox Sign), CRM built for agencies (promoters, clients, venues, auto-fill across bookings), artist media kit builder, commission/revenue reporting, Artist App (offline access, tour schedules, documents).

**Target**: Booking agencies, artist managers, promoters. Covers orchestras, touring DJs, party bands, solo acts.

**Pricing**:
- Starts with 2 included agent licenses, pay for additional seats
- Unlimited artist profiles at no extra cost
- Optional upgrades: API access, advanced Artist App
- No public monthly price; one estimate: ~$1,210 per full license
- No hidden fees, no long-term contracts

**Key strengths vs Hour**: 15 years of maturity. 55k agents and artists in platform. Strong in UK + Europe. Explicitly mentions orchestras and performing arts. Custom document engine.

**Key weakness vs Hour**: Still agency-perspective software. No production management, tech rider, rehearsal scheduling. No company-internal operations.

**Traction**: 55k agents/artists is substantial. 15 years = established, not going away.

**Threat level**: HIGH. UK/EU presence, performing arts coverage, mature product. The incumbent in EU booking agency software.

## 4. Gap analysis — Where is the whitespace?

### The central gap Hour occupies

**No existing tool serves a mid-size European performing arts company from difusion through production and invoicing.** Every competitor falls into one of these buckets:

1. **Booking agency tools** (SystemOne, ABOSS, Overture, Stagent, Gigwell, Prism.fm, Muzeek): Designed for the *agent who books artists for venues*. The user is an intermediary. A performing arts company doing its own booking outreach has to awkwardly adapt agency vocabulary. None handle production management, tech riders, or internal ops.

2. **Production/scheduling tools** (Propared, Master Tour, StageSwift): Handle logistics and scheduling but NOT the booking/outreach/difusion phase. You need to already have confirmed dates. No CRM, no sales pipeline, no contract negotiation workflow.

3. **Venue-side platforms** (Artifax, Momentus, Tessitura, Spektrix): The company is a *supplier* to these platforms, not a user. These manage the venue's operations, not the company's.

4. **Labor compliance** (Ares): Spanish-specific HR/payroll. No outreach, no production, no multi-country.

5. **Festival management** (FestivalPro, Eventotron): Festival-specific workflows. Companies are suppliers to festivals.

### Specific whitespace dimensions

| Gap | Who has it? | Hour's answer |
|-----|------------|---------------|
| **Booking outreach FROM the company's perspective** (not the agent's) | Nobody. Agency tools assume you're booking *artists*. | Engagement entity with outreach workflow |
| **Difusion + production in one tool** | Nobody. It's either CRM or production, never both. | Full funnel: engagement → show → line → invoice |
| **European performing arts vocabulary** | Only Ares (Spanish only). Agency tools speak "music industry." | Anti-CRM vocabulary: person, engagement, show, venue |
| **Tech rider management** (stage plot, light plot, sound patch) | Nobody in SaaS. Companies use Google Drive folders. | Room-level assets with versioning |
| **Mid-size European pricing** (25-120 €/mo) | Ares (30-60 €). Agency tools start at 99-131 €/mo but for the wrong user. | 25/60/120 €/mo tiered |
| **Multi-genre** (theatre + dance + music + circus + spoken word) | StageSwift partially. Most tools are music-only. | Genre-agnostic "Room" concept |
| **i18n from day one** | Only Ares (Catalan/Spanish docs). Agency tools are English-only. | Full app i18n |

### The competitive moat

Hour's defensibility comes from sitting at the **intersection** of two established categories that nobody else bridges:

```
              BOOKING/CRM              PRODUCTION
              (agency tools)           (scheduling tools)
                    ↘                ↙
                      [  HOUR  ]
                    ↗                ↖
              COMPANY perspective     EU / multi-genre
              (not agent)            (not US / music-only)
```

The closest threats would be:
1. **Stagent or ABOSS** expanding from agency → company perspective and adding production features
2. **StageSwift** adding a booking outreach CRM
3. **Ares** building a real difusion module (unlikely given their team/focus)

None of these seem imminent based on current product trajectories.

## 5. Strategic takeaways

1. **The real competition is NOT other tools — it's spreadsheets and email.** Most mid-size European performing arts companies manage difusion via Excel + email + WhatsApp + Google Drive. Hour's true competitor is inertia.

2. **Agency tools are the benchmark for booking UX** but serve the wrong user. Study SystemOne, ABOSS, and Overture's contract/advancing/settlement workflows. Adapt for company perspective.

3. **Ares remains the only direct competitor** in the Spanish performing arts niche, but their product scope is narrow (labor compliance) and their traction signals are weak. The competitive window is wider than initially assumed.

4. **StageSwift is the closest conceptual match** — performing arts touring companies, UK-based, covers scheduling + logistics + invoicing. But it's small, lacks booking outreach, and has no visible traction. Worth monitoring.

5. **Stagent's Artwin partnership is the most concerning signal.** Artwin is an established Dutch booking platform. If the joint venture expands beyond music agencies toward performing arts companies, they'd be well-positioned in EU with proper invoicing/contracts.

6. **Master Tour is the UX/logistics benchmark** for tour management features. Their $65/mo price point for a single TM license + unlimited crew access is a smart model to study.

7. **Pricing sweet spot confirmed**: 25-120 €/mo puts Hour below agency tools (which start at ~€99-131/mo for the wrong user) and alongside Ares's range. The 120 €/mo PRO tier competes on features that nobody else offers in this segment.

8. **Phase 1 positioning line**: "The first tool built for the performing arts company that books, produces, and tours its own work."
